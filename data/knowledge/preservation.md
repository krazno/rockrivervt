## overview

Rock River Preservation, Inc. is a volunteer-run nonprofit formed in 2005 (emerging from earlier volunteer organizing in the early 2000s) to conserve publicly accessible recreation along Rock River in southern Vermont. Work includes land protection, trail and shoreline stewardship, steward programs, and coordination with neighbors and towns (Newfane and Dummerston).

## notes

Documented milestones on this site’s History page include: nonprofit reorganization with 501(c)(3) status and a management plan focus (2003–2006 era); purchase of roughly 4.5 acres of riverfront on March 16, 2007, with conservation protections held by the Vermont Land Trust; Tropical Storm Irene impacts and volunteer recovery (August 2011); conversations leading to purchase of about 21.32 additional acres, closing November 20, 2018, with broad community fundraising. The Preservation page summarizes easement, deed restriction, and management-plan themes (including parking shifts associated with Town of Newfane barriers and public parking along Route 30 near Depot Road in Dummerston). Totals and plan years on the live site should stay in sync with official nonprofit publications.

## visitor context

Public access is a shared privilege: follow posted rules, stay off private land without permission, pack out trash, and treat stewards as partners. Formal volunteer onboarding and policies belong to Rock River Preservation—not the RockRiverVT volunteer website.
