## overview

Rock River has long drawn a mixed crowd: families, swimmers, hikers, and others sharing woodland trails and river pools. Some stretches include signed clothing-optional areas that are part of local custom; expectations are posted on site. The ethic emphasized across this guide is mutual respect, modest sound, consent before photos, leave-no-trace habits, and giving others space.

## notes

This knowledge file should stay observational—not promotional, not explicit, and not activist sloganeering. Steward volunteers and Rock River Preservation help align behavior with posted rules; the nonprofit is the authority for formal policy. RockRiverVT is a neighbor-maintained field guide, not a government or nonprofit mouthpiece.

## visitor context

First-time visitors benefit from reading Guidelines before arriving. When in doubt, default to quieter behavior, smaller groups, and carrying out trash. Mixed use works best when everyone treats the river as shared infrastructure, not a private venue.

## cautions

Do not use coded or sexualized language. Do not promise illegality or legality—point to posted rules and Guidelines. Avoid stereotypes about any group of visitors.
