## overview

Rock River’s recent history on this site is told as volunteer-led conservation: people organizing cleanups and alerts, then formalizing as a nonprofit, purchasing land with easements, weathering Irene, and expanding protected acreage through a community fundraising effort. The full narrative and tone live on the History page; this file is the knowledge-base summary for reuse elsewhere.

## notes

Anchor events repeated for editors (verify against official nonprofit history before changing): Summer 2000 — users organize The Rock River Preservation Society; 2003–2006 — reorganization toward Rock River Preservation, Inc. with IRS 501(c)(3) and management planning; March 16, 2007 — ~4.5 acre riverfront purchase with Vermont Land Trust-held conservation protections; August 27–28, 2011 — Tropical Storm Irene; 2014 — conversations with landowners of heavily used swimming areas across from organization land; Summer–fall 2018 — community fundraising (200+ contributors, >$90,000 cited on History page); November 20, 2018 — ~21.32 acre purchase closes. Later management-plan updates on the Preservation page (2021, 2023) must match nonprofit documents.
